Disaster Help & Precautions

Choose a Disaster to Learn More

Earthquake

Earthquakes can occur without warning. It is important to be prepared and follow safety precautions to minimize damage and injuries.

Precautions:

  • Drop, cover, and hold on during the shaking.
  • Stay indoors during shaking; avoid windows and heavy furniture.
  • If outside, move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines.
  • If driving, pull over and remain in the car until shaking stops.
  • After the shaking stops, check for hazards like gas leaks and electrical damage.

Storm

Storms, particularly severe ones, can bring heavy rains, strong winds, and lightning. Being prepared is essential to protect yourself and your property.

Precautions:

  • Stay indoors and away from windows during the storm.
  • If possible, move to a lower floor to avoid flooding.
  • Keep emergency supplies, including a flashlight and battery-powered radio, in case of power outages.
  • Avoid using electrical appliances during the storm to prevent electric shock.
  • After the storm, avoid standing water and check for downed power lines.

Cyclone

Cyclones are powerful storms with high winds and heavy rainfall. Cyclones can cause massive damage, so it's important to be prepared and follow evacuation orders if necessary.

Precautions:

  • Stay tuned to weather updates and evacuation notices.
  • Secure windows and doors to minimize damage from strong winds.
  • If you live in a flood-prone area, evacuate before the cyclone arrives.
  • Store emergency supplies such as food, water, and first aid kits.
  • Avoid coastal areas and low-lying regions at risk of storm surges.

Flood

Flooding can happen after heavy rainfall or when rivers overflow. It can lead to widespread damage. It's important to stay informed and take precautions if flooding is imminent.

Precautions:

  • If you live in a flood-prone area, know your evacuation routes.
  • Keep a disaster kit with essentials such as water, food, and medication in a waterproof container.
  • Move to higher ground immediately if flooding begins or is expected.
  • Avoid driving through flooded roads as they may be unsafe.
  • After a flood, stay away from standing water to avoid contamination.
  • Have an emergency kit with supplies like food, water, medication, and important documents.
  • Stay informed about potential disasters in your area through news, weather apps, and official alerts.
  • Create a family emergency plan, including meeting points and communication methods.
